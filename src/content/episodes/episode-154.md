# Episode 154 Starting a Pentesting Company on Top of Bug Bounty

### TL;DR
- Joseph and Brandyn discuss transitioning from bug bounty to pentesting for income diversification
- Pentesting smooths out bug bounty's "spiky" income; provides mental consistency
- Key advantages for bug hunters: external proof of quality (public reports, brand logos), higher skill ceiling vs average pentesters
- Pricing strategies: use bug bounty hourly rate as floor; charge per test not per hour; US market pays ~2x UK

### Key Takeaways
- **Diversification**: Pentesting provides guaranteed cash vs bug bounty's uncertainty
- **Inbound leads**: Build personal brand (talks, write-ups, X) → clients reach out → no cold sales needed
- **Pricing**: Know your bug bounty hourly rate; use as floor for pentest pricing. US market pays significantly more than UK
- **Sales conversations**: Be up-front about your value; mention bug bounty earnings/brands as proof of quality
- **Delivery is 50-70% of the job**: The rest is client communication, report writing, proposal creation; not everyone likes this
- **Retainers**: A 3-4 day/month retainer covers annual bills — frees remaining time for research/bug bounty
- **Platform option**: Pentest-as-a-service platforms (H1/Bugcrowd) take a cut but handle sales; independent selling keeps more margin
- **Compliance**: SOC 2, CREST (UK) — subcontract through larger firms if needed
- **AI hedge**: Hackbots will commoditize low-level bug finding; pentesting for local businesses is more defensible

### Suggestions and Advices from Hunter
- Brandyn: "Definitely, definitely, definitely pay to get the advice or consult someone that is very well versed in business and tax structure."
- Brandyn: "Pen testing isn't bug bounty but paid more. It's 50-70% hacking and 30% delivery, soft skills, client communication, writing proposals."
- Joseph: "Use your bug bounty findings as proof of your quality. If you've made over $100K doing bug bounty, people will be impressed."
