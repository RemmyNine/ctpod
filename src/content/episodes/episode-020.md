# Episode 20 Hacker Brain Hacks — Overcoming Bug Bounty's Mental Tolls

**Guests/Hosts:** Justin Gardner, Joel Margolis  
**Date:** 2023-05-25 | **Duration:** 1:06:30

### TL;DR
- Full episode on the psychological challenges of bug bounty: before/during/after hacking
- Procrastination Education (tutorial hell), Analysis Paralysis (which program to pick), Automation Obsession
- Imposter syndrome never goes away — treat it as part of the process
- Set goals, know your threat model, avoid low-impact rabbit holes
- Burnout prevention: find identity outside bug bounty, take breaks, exercise

### Key Takeaways
- **Before hacking:** Fight procrastination by just starting; pick programs that interest you, not just high bounties; avoid over-investing in automation before you know how to hack
- **During hacking:** Stay in the zone — take notes on things to automate later, do not switch to coding mid-hunt; to avoid bad rabbit holes, check: does it expand scope? Is impact clear? Is it deterministic?
- **After hacking:** Expect burnout; separate self-worth from bounty results; take real time off between intense periods
- Use the "no-bug time limit" — e.g., 3 hours per target section, then move on

### Techniques and Primitives
- **Goal-setting with tiers** — Set financial goals (e.g., hot tub at $X, cruise at $Y) to maintain motivation during live hacking events
- **Document everything** — 15-minute brain dump at end of session; use HackMD or local markdown
- **Persist iteratively** — When stuck, re-run the same attack vectors; the answer often comes after multiple mental iterations

### Tooling and Resources
- PortSwigger Web Security Academy — beginner training
- HackMD — collaborative note-taking
- "How to Pick a Good Bug Bounty Program" — Episode 13

### Suggestions and Advices from Hunter
- Justin: "For every hour you waste right now trying to hack this or not finding anything, that's another hour you're not going to have to spend before you find your own vulnerability."
- From Hacklook's tips thread: target fresh programs, use automation wisely, read other hunters' reports, engage the community.
- "Stay curious. Don't be focused too much on bounties or findings. Be focused on becoming an expert on the application."

#### 8. Red flags and pitfalls
- **Giving up too early** — Many bugs require creative pivoting when initial exploitation paths are blocked
- **Ignoring blind vulnerabilities** — Blind SSRF, blind XSS, and timing-based attacks can be just as impactful as visible ones
- **Missing the forest for the trees** — Don't get tunnel vision on one vulnerability class; step back and consider the full attack surface

#### 9. Vocabulary
- **Bug Bounty** — Program where companies reward researchers for finding security vulnerabilities
- **Responsible Disclosure** — Reporting vulnerabilities to vendors before public disclosure
- **Attack Surface** — All points where an unauthorized user can try to enter or extract data
