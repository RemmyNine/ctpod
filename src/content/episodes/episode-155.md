# Episode 155 2025 Hacker Stats & 2026 Goals

### TL;DR
- Year-end retrospective: Justin (104 bugs, 22.27 avg impact), Joseph (100 bugs, 22.85 avg), Brandyn (160+ bugs, 21.5 avg)
- Justin hit first six-figure bounty; Joseph's highlight was Tokyo LHE + Amazon IPC best bug
- Collaboration discussion: Joseph realizes he performs better with team vs solo
- Justin retiring from conference talks (hates slide prep); will do podcasts/hackalongs instead
- AI-powered manual hacking: SHIFT agents in Kaido for automated research

### Key Takeaways
- **Report everything meaningful** — Brandyn's mindset shift to full-time: submit every valid attack vector regardless of severity
- **Google report quality bonus**: POC videos with realistic-looking UI dramatically increase bounties; explain attack in text too (triagers/devs/security all have different needs)
- **CVSS for AI bugs is broken**: Too much "user interaction required" — Joseph plans to write a reference doc
- **Collaboration > solo**: Joseph's epiphany — collaborative hacking is more motivating, more fun, and more profitable
- **Three anchor programs** (Google, Epic, Capital One) provide consistent depth
- **SHIFT agents** in Kaido for automated research: point at specific anomaly, get 3-4 insights not previously known

### Suggestions and Advices from Hunter

- Brandyn: "Know your target and adapt. I'm very adaptive to my target because I want to find bugs no matter what."
