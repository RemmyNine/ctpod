# Episode 87 'Hacker Wife' Mariah Gardner on Bug Bounty Mentality and Relationships

**Full ASR transcript** — This is a lifestyle/relationship episode rather than a technical hunting episode. Mariah covers:
- Her journey from not understanding bug bounty to becoming a key support system
- The decision to go full-time bug bounty (financial margin, track record, risk management)
- Managing isolation, motivation, and self-worth separate from bounty performance
- Goal/reward system: tiered prizes for live hacking event performance (from small appliances to hot tubs)
- Balancing life with a newborn while maintaining bug bounty income
- Advice for partners: communicate boundaries, batch interruptions, give full attention when present
- The "not working spiral" — how to recognize when you're reevaluating based on bad data

**Key Takeaways**
- Track record + financial margin + back-up employability = zero-risk transition to full-time
- If you're questioning your bug bounty career, audit your actual hours worked first — often it's underwork, not lack of skill
- Goal systems that gamify hacking performance (tiered rewards) sustain motivation better than pure financial tracking
- Communicate work boundaries clearly: "off at 6pm" means 6pm unless renegotiated in advance
- Batch interruptions: collect 3 questions before interrupting flow state

#### 6. Mental models
- **Think like an attacker** — Always consider the worst-case impact of a vulnerability. What would a malicious actor do with this access?
- **Persistence pays off** — The best bugs often come from spending deep time on a single target rather than skimming many targets.
- **Follow the data** — Track how user input flows through the application. Sources (inputs) lead to sinks (dangerous operations).

#### 8. Red flags and pitfalls
- **Giving up too early** — Many bugs require creative pivoting when initial exploitation paths are blocked

- **Missing the forest for the trees** — Don't get tunnel vision on one vulnerability class; step back and consider the full attack surface
